Concept

An attempt at an alternative setting importing Oriental (specifically Japanese) culture into a playable world to be used in conjunction with the rules from the Vampire the Masqquerade 1st Edition Players Handbook.

Players roll Oriental based characters to play as semi-immortal humans who can change into forms of a Dragon.

Gameplay Changes

Full rules from the World of Darkness with a number of customizations.

  • Dragons are not Vampires but use some of the same Disciplines
  • Blood Pool points are replaced with Gnosis points.
  • Draconis is an additional specialized discipline available to all characters.
  • Dragon clans replaced the traditional vampire clans.

Dragon Breeds

The breeds of the drãgons are two: homid, and drãgon. They mix freely and get along quite well, but there is that air of superiority emenating from the drãgon breed, as they hold many of the most influential and powerful positions. The drãgons have six clans: black, blue, red, white, gold, and green. Each clan is profoundly different and usually stay to their own, but are honored when gived the chance to mix with others colors. All drãgons are of Japanese stock and are prepared for the honor when they are very young. Some families of drãgons are considered reverant and are indeed groomed for positions of power.

  • HOMID - born of a human parent, beginning Gnosis 3
  • DRAGON - born of a dragon parent, beginning Gnosis 5
Dragon Forms

There are three forms of the drãgon: homid, drãconian, and drãgon. Each form takes 1 full round to transform into, after rolling STA+Primal Urge, difficulty of the form. A drãgon of homid breed has a 5 difficulty to transform into homid form, likewise for those born a drãgon.

  • HOMID - no trait adjustment
  • DRACONIAN - This form is a mixture of man and drãgon, and is often quite horrible and terrifying to look at. Each form is unique and can be modified by spending time meditating and practicing. STR+3, DEX+2, STA+3, PER+2; DIFF 7
  • DRAGON - This form is also unique and varies greatly from drãgon to drãgon, from the small, fast scouts, to the huge, imposing clan leaders. Size depends on rank, each drãgon increses by 1/8 for each level of rank. STR+5, DEX+6, STA+5, CLAW+3, BITE+4, TAIL+2, can fly 20mph per rank, and gets +5 to soak.
Dragon Language

The drãgon language is old and complicated, and its secrets are closely guarded. The written language is a mixture of Japanes style ideograms, and ancient drãgons runes, and the spoken language is even moreso misunderstood, and hard to learn. They also have a language for the nungen,or mortals, which incorporates some characters from this language.
